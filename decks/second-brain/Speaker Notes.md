# Speaker Notes & Presentation Guide

## Overview
- **Duration:** 10 minutes
- **Audience:** OVOU team (mixed technical/non-technical)
- **Format:** Slidev presentation, online company call
- **Core message:** A simple folder structure reduces decision fatigue. AI tools make it effortless to maintain. Start small, iterate, don't wait for perfect.

## Slide Flow (14 slides)

| # | Slide | Time | Key Point |
|---|-------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Title | — | — |
| 2 | "How many times..." | ~30s | Let the question land. Pause. |
| 3 | 10 places | ~1 min | Walk through the icons, make it relatable |
| 4 | One place first | ~30s | Transition to PARA |
| 5 | PARA in 10 seconds | ~1 min | Keep it light, don't lecture |
| 6 | Decision fatigue | ~30s | The "why" behind the method |
| 7 | Before: my mess | ~30s | Self-deprecating, relatable |
| 8 | I tried PARA | ~30s | Honest — it helped but wasn't perfect |
| 9 | Made it simpler | ~1.5 min | **Show vault screenshot here.** Your evolved structure. |
| 10 | The mindset | ~1 min | **Key slide.** Iterate, don't procrastinate for perfect. |
| 11 | AI transition | ~20s | Brief bridge |
| 12 | Performance review | ~1 min | Concrete proof story |
| 13 | AI as facilitator | ~30s | Capture, Organize, Retrieve |
| 14 | Start small | ~1 min | CTA — one inbox, iterate |

## Tips
- Slide 2: Don't rush. The silence after the question is what makes it land.
- Slide 9: Have Obsidian sidebar screenshot ready (or embed in slide). Point to Inbox, Knowledge subfolders, Projects, Archive.
- Slide 10: This is your strongest message. Slow down here.
- Slide 12: Make it personal. "I had to fill MY review" not "you could use it for reviews."
- Slide 14: End warm and encouraging, not preachy.
